This can increase the likelihood of them seeking help when needed. While children understand the concept of death at a very young age, they may not fully comprehend the complex social and personal factors that lead to someone’s death. It is important for teachers, school counsellors and parents to provide as much information and resources to your child as possible so they can understand the challenges people face, and when, why and how to help. There are resources available, including a section in the Canadian Association for Suicide Prevention's publication, Responding to a Crisis, which provides information and helpful strategies for kids ages 7-12.
